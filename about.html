About AP-GAINED

AP-GAINED is a capacity-building initiative designed to empower National Research and Education Networks (NRENs) throughout the Asia Pacific region to effectively harness the power of Generative AI (GenAI). The project will primarily target NRENs in Least Developed Countries (LDCs) and Lower Middle-Income Countries (LMICs) while creating a model applicable to the broader community.

Through pilot studies, a comprehensive GenAI curriculum, practical training, and establishing an NREN GenAI Community, the project aims to enhance NREN efficiency, expand services, accelerate research, and foster a skilled technical workforce that promotes social inclusion.

This project will bridge the capacity gap faced by many NRENs, enabling them to better serve their research and education communities, drive innovation across the Asia Pacific region, bridge the digital divide, and promote gender equality and social inclusion.

Project Background

National Research and Education Networks (NRENs) play a vital role in driving innovation and fostering education, particularly within Least Developed Countries (LDCs) and Lower Middle-Income Countries (LMICs). However, many NRENs face significant challenges in developing their capacity due to limited funding, difficulty in attracting qualified staff, and the need to maximize the potential of their existing workforce. These hurdles impede NRENs in fulfilling their mission to support research, teaching, and collaboration within their communities.

The emergence of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) presents a transformative opportunity to address these capacity challenges. GenAI tools offer powerful capabilities in content generation, data analysis, code assistance, translation, and more. By harnessing GenAI, NREN staff can streamline workflows, automate tasks, and gain insights, driving productivity gains and allowing them to focus on higher-value activities.

Unfortunately, many NRENs, especially those in LDCs and LMICs, lack the resources and expertise to integrate GenAI into their operations effectively. Moreover, the rapidly evolving nature of GenAI technologies demands continuous learning and adaptation, further straining resources.

This project seeks to bridge this gap by developing a scalable and tailored capacity development program focused on empowering NREN staff across countries to use GenAI effectively in their work. It will prioritize the needs of LDCs and LMICs while offering a model applicable to NRENs globally.

We are aware of ongoing projects addressing NREN capacity development, including those focused on technical skills and infrastructure. However, a dedicated program focused on the potential of GenAI represents a crucial and unique next step. There is substantial evidence of the impact of these capacity limitations. NRENs in LDCs and LMICs often struggle to meaningfully support cutting-edge research, deliver innovative educational services, and realize their full potential as drivers of development.

Project Goals

This project aims to empower NREN staff across the Asia Pacific region to effectively integrate Generative AI (GenAI) technologies into their workflows, unlocking productivity gains, streamlining operations, and enhancing their services to researchers, educators, and students.

Key Outcomes

  • Tailored GenAI Curriculum and Training Material: A modular curriculum offering foundational AI knowledge for all NREN staff, as well as specialized modules for technical, research support, and administrative roles.
  • NREN Staff AI Capacity: Develop a comprehensive GenAI capacity development program tailored to the unique needs of NRENs, prioritizing the challenges faced by those in LDCs and LMICs.
  • Practical Skills Development: Hands-on training and projects allowing NREN staff to apply GenAI tools to core NREN functions, including network management, research support, educational outreach, and administrative tasks.
  • Open Educational Resource (OER): Create a repository of NREN-specific GenAI use cases, best practices, and resources, promoting the ongoing adoption and innovation surrounding GenAI technology.
  • Stronger NRENs: Empower NRENs, particularly in LDCs and LMICs, to use GenAI for automating tasks, gaining data insights, and delivering innovative services, to drive educational advancement, research breakthroughs, and digital transformation in their countries.

Anticipated Benefits for Internet Development and/or Technical Community

  • Enhanced NREN Efficiency: NRENs will achieve greater productivity through process automation and informed decision-making powered by GenAI, allowing for better resource allocation. Staff empowered with GenAI tools can develop new, innovative services to support research and education, especially in under-resourced regions.
  • Empowered Technical Community: GenAI adoption by technical staff within NRENs will lead to streamlined network operations, proactive maintenance, and optimized resource utilization, ensuring the robust and efficient backbone of the research and education network.
  • Up-skilling and Continuous Learning: The project nurtures technical talent within NRENs, equipping staff with cutting-edge AI skills applicable across networking domains. The community of practice fosters knowledge sharing and helps technical personnel stay ahead in the rapidly evolving field.

Project Plan

AP-GAINED employs a multi-pronged and collaborative approach to empower NRENs with GenAI capabilities. Our carefully designed project plan leverages a train-the-trainer model, pilot programs for grounded refinement, regional knowledge and skill exchange workshops, and the development of sustainable online resources. This comprehensive strategy ensures both immediate capacity gains and creates a foundation for long-term adoption of GenAI within NRENs across the Asia Pacific region.
